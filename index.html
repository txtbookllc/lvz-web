How much does it cost?

Low Vision Zoom is free to try for 7 days with every feature unlocked. After that, a one-time $9.99 license keeps it running — no subscription, and all future updates are included. See the pricing page.

What happens when the trial ends?

The app keeps working — magnification is simply limited until you enter a license key, so your screen always stays readable enough to navigate and buy. Enter a key any time to restore full zoom instantly; no reinstall needed.

How is this different from the built-in Windows Magnifier?

Windows includes a capable magnifier, and many people should try it first. Low Vision Zoom focuses on a smoother, calmer feel: gentle animated zoom (no snapping), zoom centered on your cursor, steady dead-zone panning, and a stay-out-of-the-way tray interface for quick, frequent adjustments.

Will Windows warn me when I install it?

The installer is Authenticode-signed, which clears SmartScreen “unknown publisher” warnings as the signing reputation builds. It installs per-machine to Program Files, which is also what lets clicks land correctly while zoomed.

Does my purchase data stay private?

Yes. The app itself collects nothing and works offline (privacy policy). Purchases are handled by our payment provider, Paddle, who acts as the merchant of record; the app never sees your payment details.
